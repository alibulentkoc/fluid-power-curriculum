# Module 05 — Power Units

> **The machine:** the **Precision Hydraulic Lift Platform** — raises a load to a commanded height and holds it to within ±1 mm.
> **What this module does:** Module 04 characterised the cylinder — how much force and speed it gives, and how it behaves in motion. All of that assumed fluid arrives at 100 bar and 10 L/min. Module 05 builds the thing that *makes* that fluid: the power unit.

---

## Where you are in the build

You have a fully characterised cylinder and a fully specified fluid, but nothing yet to pressurise and move that fluid. Module 04 ended with a demand — the cylinder needs about **10 L/min at 100 bar** to lift the load in about seven seconds. Module 05 meets that demand by specifying the **hydraulic power unit**: the pump that makes the flow, the electric motor that drives the pump, the relief valve that caps the pressure, and the reservoir that holds and conditions the oil.

Each lesson takes one part of the power unit, states the decision it forces, and settles it with the platform's own demand.

1. **What the power unit must supply** — the flow, pressure, and power the unit must deliver. *Decision: the demand, and the four parts to meet it.*
2. **The pump** — the component that turns shaft rotation into flow. *Decision: pump displacement and type.*
3. **The electric motor** — the prime mover that drives the pump. *Decision: motor power rating.*
4. **The relief valve** — the safety limit on pressure. *Decision: the relief setting.*
5. **The reservoir and the complete unit** — holding, cooling, and de-aerating the oil. *Decision: reservoir size, and the assembled power unit.*

By the end you will have a specified power unit — pump, motor, relief, and reservoir — ready for Module 06 to control with valves.
