# Module 07 — Circuits

> **The machine:** the **Precision Hydraulic Lift Platform** — raises a load to a commanded height and holds it to within ±1 mm.
> **What this module does:** Modules 05 and 06 built the power unit and the valve set. Module 07 wires them together into the **complete hydraulic circuit** — the single schematic that shows how every part connects, how the oil flows to raise, lower, and hold, and where every bar of pressure goes.

---

## Where you are in the build

You now hold every component: a reservoir, pump, motor, relief valve, and filter (the power unit, Module 05); and a proportional directional valve, a counterbalance valve, and the cylinder (the valve set and actuator, Modules 04 and 06). Loose, they are a parts bin. This module connects them — and to do that, you first learn to *read* the standard drawing that engineers use to describe a hydraulic system, then assemble the platform's own circuit and trace the oil through it.

Each lesson takes one part of that job, states the decision it forces, and settles it with the platform's own parts and numbers.

1. **Reading a hydraulic circuit** — the ISO 1219 symbol language and how a schematic maps the machine. *Decision: how the circuit is represented.*
2. **Assembling the platform circuit** — connecting power unit, directional valve, counterbalance valve, and cylinder. *Decision: how the parts connect.*
3. **Tracing the operating states** — following the oil for raise, lower, and hold. *Decision: what each state does.*
4. **The pressure and flow budget** — accounting for every drop from supply to cylinder and back. *Decision: where the pressure goes.*
5. **The complete circuit and fail-safe behaviour** — the finished schematic and how the load stays held under faults. *Decision: the complete, safe circuit.*

By the end you will have the platform's complete hydraulic circuit — drawn, connected, traced, and understood — ready for Module 08 to add electrical sensing and control.
